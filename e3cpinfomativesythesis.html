Gender Secrecy Ruling

        California gender secrecy policies have been ruled unconstitutional by US District judge Roger Benitez due to violation of constitutional rights of parents and teachers who desired to inform parents of their children's gender identity. This story was covered by both the San Francisco Chronicle and the Christian Broadcasting Network, both with very differing points of view over the ruling. Both CBN and SF Chronicle discuss the ruling in the class-action lawsuit Mirabelli, et al. v. Olson, et al.  represented by the Thomas More Society, and what it meant for California schools.

        CBN’s article author, Benjamin Gill, wrote about the judge Benitez’s decision and their word choice on why he ruled against the gender secrecy policies in California. This was further supported in the title of his article, “’Children Do Not Belong to the Government': Federal Judge Smacks Down CA Gender Secrecy Policies”, quoting Greg Burt of the California Family Council, who agreed with the judge’s ruling. CBN have been a more right-leaning network, which is why they are against the ruling and use strong negative words like ‘deceive’ and ‘force’ in the first sentence of the article, “California public schools can no longer deceive parents or force teachers to keep parents in the dark”, further solidifying their position against gender secrecy policies.

        San Francisco Chronicles, or SF Chronicles is a more left-leaning newspaper that focuses on what occurs in California, demonstrated by their softer word choice for gender identity as ‘transgender’ and the use of selective qualifying adjectives like ‘emergency motion’ (Egelko). The author, Egelko, also included quotes from Bonta’s office deputy solicitor general Julie Veroff, ‘Outing transgender students to their parents before they are ready threatens

severe mental and emotional anguish, depression, and in extreme cases, even

suicide.’ The quote is used to add urgency into their side, making the ruling look dangerous to the youth that it is involving in California, and suggesting the reason why the ruling is in bad taste.

        The intended audience of CBN is right-leaning christians while SF Chronicle’s audience is more inclined towards left-leaning egalitarians, proven by their article titles use of words like ‘gender identification’ (Egelko) or ‘Smacks Down CA Gender Secrecy Policies’ (Gill). This leads to both articles being extremely biased and painting completely different perspectives on what the parents or child's constitutional rights are. SF Chronicle believes that the child’s privacy is being breached by this ruling and may cause harm to them, while CBN believes that the parents are being treated as ‘harassers from whom students need to be protected from.’

        Ultimately, both of the articles go over US District judge Roger Benitez ruling against gender secrecy policies in California, but have different reactions to the decision. CBN has more quotes from parents and includes a quote from the Thomas More Society representing their side, while the San Francisco Chronicle includes quotes from their representatives Bonta’s Office and State Superintendent of Public Instruction Tony Thurmond regarding the policy's existence.
