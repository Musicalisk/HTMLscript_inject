Analysis on Advertisements

The first advertisement is for Vitamin Water, and it’s intended for young athletes. The label on the bottle includes facts, and the text at the bottom next describes Kobe Bryant's achievements since he started drinking the product? The appeal used is ethos and a bit of logos due to Kobe Bryant being a team sports player that used the product, and has some of the nutrition facts on the label of the product showing.

The second advertisement is for Toshiba’s Satellite series Notebook laptop, and it’s intended for office workers. The product is having its specifications listed, and Toshiba uses the company Intel for their CPU. The appeal is ethos because the product uses its own credibility of being manufactured by a computer manufacturer, and in addition also boasts of running off of Intel’s CPU, a company that makes computer hardware.

The third advertisement is for Coca-Cola’s drink, and it’s intended for a perverted male audience. The product is being held by a female in swim apparel with the top left corner mentioning about the Summer.  The appeal is pathos due to the assumption that the audience likes scantily dressed females, making them feel more at ease with the product.

The fourth advertisement is for Greenpeace’s tissues, and it’s intended for Canadians susceptible to allergies. The product is being displayed with facts about the product at the bottom and instructions on how to use the product. The appeal being used is logos because the facts are being parsed in a logical fashion, and it includes instructions for replicating results.

The fifth advertisement is for Levi’s clothing, and it’s intended for manual labor workers. The advertisement is displaying a worker using the clothing with giant bold text declaring “This country was not built by men in suits.” The appeal is pathos as it invokes the emotion of the reader to connect Levi’s clothing to hard workers.

The sixth and final advertisement is for Lancôme’s Idôle perfume, and it is intended for a female audience. The advertisement shows the product being held by a female. This uses pathos as it makes the audience feel that the only good choice for perfume is Lancôme’s product.
